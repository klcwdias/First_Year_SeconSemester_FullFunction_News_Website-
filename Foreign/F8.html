UKRAINE WAR: RUSSIA ATTACKS THEATRE SHELTERING CIVILIANS, MARIUPOL SAYS


According to local reports, Russian forces detonated a bomb on a theatre in the besieged city of Mariupol where civilians were being housed. According to Deputy Mayor Sergei Orlov, between 1,000 and 1,200 people sought sanctuary in the structure. The exact number of those killed or injured is still unknown. The information could not be independently verified by the BBC. A maternity hospital, a church, and apartment blocks have all been struck by Russian warplanes and shells.

Local authorities estimate that at least 2,400 people have died in Mariupol since the war began, while they admit that this figure is likely to be underestimated. Thousands of people have been buried in mass graves. An estimated 300,000 people are stranded in the city, which has been cut off from running water, electricity, and gas. Food and water supplies are depleting as a result of Russian troops refusing to allow humanitarian aid to be delivered. In a statement, Mariupol's municipal council stated Russian soldiers "deliberately and shamelessly demolished" the theatre, adding that a "plane dropped a bomb on a structure where hundreds of peaceful Mariupol people were hiding." Because the city was still being shelled, the scope of the onslaught was still unknown, according to the statement. Smoke billowed from the structure, with the façade completely fallen, according to a photo given by the city council and verified by the BBC. Dmytro Kuleba, Ukraine's foreign minister, called it "another horrifying war crime in Mariupol," adding that "Russians could not have realized this was a civilian shelter."

According to the BBC, many children and elderly people were taking refuge inside, and the situation was rapidly escalating. Maxar, a US company, revealed satellite images obtained on March 14 that showed the word "children" inscribed in Russian on the sidewalk outside the structure, according to the company. According to the Russian news agency RIA, the Russian defense ministry denied carrying out an air strike against the theater just hours after the news broke. According to Mr. Orlov, the deputy mayor, about 1,500 cars managed to escape Mariupol on Wednesday. However, he claimed that a Russian attack on the convoy left at least five people wounded, including a kid. The president of the International Committee of the Red Cross, Peter Maurer, appealed for improved access to civilians caught up in the conflict, which he described as creating "enormous suffering." Mr. Maurer, who is visiting Ukraine for five days, called the situation in Mariupol a "waking nightmare."

At least ten persons standing in line for food in Chernihiv, Ukraine's northern city, were murdered by Russian shelling, according to the country's prosecutor general. A local news channel aired unconfirmed footage of bodies on the street.

Ben King(Business reporter, BBC News) 17-Mar-2022 5
