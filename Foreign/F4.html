Pensioner detained after mutilated body found


A retiree has been charged with murder and dismemberment of a 68-year-old lady she met online. An adult female torso was discovered in a bag inside a shopping trolley near Harvey Marcelin's Brooklyn apartment. She's now facing charges of second-degree murder and concealing a human body.

Marcelin had already served over 50 years in prison for the murders of two female companions. On March 4, cops discovered a human head and saw blades in her residence. A leg was discovered a few streets north of the torso a few days later. Marcelin and another individual are allegedly seen preparing for the murder at a Home Depot DIY store, according to NYPD Chief of Detectives James Essig. They made a purchase.

Surveillance video from February 27 shows a lady entering Marcelin's apartment with the same suitcase in which the torso was later discovered. Susan Leyden was the victim, according to police, and she and Marcelin had known one other for around two years. According to Brooklyn North Chief John Chell, the two may have met over social media. "We are still going through how the relationship came about and what the real relationship is," he added. Marcelin - who recently identified as a woman - was convicted guilty of murdering Jacqueline Bonds by a jury in 1963. Marcelin was arrested again in 1984 after being released on parole for stabbing another girlfriend.

Manslaughter was found against her. Marcelin admitting to having "issues" with women during a State Parole Board hearing in 1997. Chief Essig says they're looking into if Marcelin was involved in any other events in the last three years. Marcelin, who was released from prison in 2019 on parole, is now being held without bail. She has also been accused with evidence tampering.
