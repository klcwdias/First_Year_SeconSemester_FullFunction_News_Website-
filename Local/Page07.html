EXPATRIATE GROUP REFUSES TO PROVIDE DOLLARS TO SRI LANKA


'Sri Lankan Expats for Change,' a group created by Sri Lankan expatriates headquartered in Milan, Italy, said today that they have chosen to stop sending money to Sri Lanka as a protest against corrupt politicians who have dominated the country in the past and presently.

"It is general known that the economic crisis in Sri Lanka was not caused by a reduction in the tourism sector or a decrease in the amount of money remitted by Lankans living abroad." It is the current rulers who have caused our family back home to endure in lengthy lines and to remain in the dark. The crooked politicians who run the country now, as well as those who reigned in the past, have squandered and looted our blood.

As a result, we, the Lankan expatriates overseas, have chosen not to send any funds to Sri Lanka until a corrupt-free administration is created," declared Sri Lankan Expatriates for Change in a statement."It's amusing to notice that they're blaming the country's current condition on falling tourist dependence and expatriate remittances." It was their incapacity to take decisive action to address Sri Lanka's economic catastrophe. "It is the government's responsibility to take substantial and tangible steps to remedy the situation," the statement continued.

"Many countries have successfully stabilized their economies following the pandemic, and it is totally unacceptable to blame the pandemic and the Ukraine-Russia war for the current perilous economic crisis in Sri Lanka," the statement said, referring to the President's address to the nation on Wednesday. Yohan Perera (Yohan Perera)
