PARLIAMENT IS ENRAGED AT BASIL'S ABSENCE


As the opposition claimed that Finance Minister Basil Rajapaksa has not made a statement in Parliament on the state of the Sri Lankan economy since December 10 last year, Speaker Mahinda Yapa Abeywardene said he has no right to compel any Minister to make a statement in the House because there are provisions for taking disciplinary action against an MP who fails to attend sessions for three months.

The Speaker made the comment after Chief Opposition Whip Lakshman Kiriella said that Minister Rajapaksa had not been in Parliament for three months to make a speech on the national economy. "Sri Lanka has become bankrupt, and the Finance Minister has not made a single statement before the House in three months." It's March 10th, and he hasn't made a comment in three months. As a result, we propose that the speaker direct the Finance Minister to appear and make a statement," Mr. Kiriella stated.

"Sri Lanka has become bankrupt, and the Finance Minister has not made a single statement before the House in three months." It's March 10th, and he hasn't made a comment in three months. As a result, we propose that the speaker direct the Finance Minister to appear and make a statement," Mr. Kiriella stated.

"House Leader Dinesh Gunawardene stated that what the Chief Opposition Whip is stating is false since the State Minister has addressed all queries posed by the opposition on behalf of the Finance Minister." "Almost every day, Mr. Kiriella makes the same argument," he stated.

According to Chief Government Whip Johnston Fernando, all remarks made by Basil Rajapaksa outside of Parliament have been considered in the House.

According to SJB MP Nalin Bandara, it is difficult to understand why Mr. Rajapaksa cannot appear in Parliament and answer questions while the Ukrainian President addresses that country's parliament despite the war situation.
