IF ARREARS ARE NOT PAID, THE WATER SUPPLY WILL BE CUT OFF, ACCORDING TO THE WATER BOARD


According to NWSDB Additional General Manager (Billing) Piyal Pathmanatha, the National Water Service and Drainage Board (NWSDB) has decided to disconnect water supply for all clients who have not completed their water bills since the onset of the COVID-19 epidemic.

He added that the NWSDB is owed Rs. 7.5 billion by its customers for providing uninterrupted water supply throughout the COVID-19 epidemic.

The NWSDB, on the other hand, has given them multiple opportunity to clear their debts. According to him, these payments would be subject to an extra 2.5 percent fee.

"Given the current scenario, the NWSDB is considering waiving Rs.1 billion in fees on consumer accounts in May 2020. To fulfill unpaid payments, the tourist industry was provided installment plans "explained Pathmanatha

Joshua Thursday, March 10, 2022, 12:26 p.m. 5
