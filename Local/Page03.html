IN SINGAPORE, A SRI LANKAN WAS RECOGNIZED FOR HALTING AN ARMED ATTACKER.


Amila Chinthana, a Sri Lankan national, was awarded the Public Spiritedness Award by the Ang Mo Kio Police Division in Singapore for holding the armed assailant and preventing him from causing more harm to the public while waiting for the police to come, according to Singapore media.

Amila Chinthana, 35, was waiting at a traffic light in Buangkok Crescent on Monday afternoon (March 14) when he was attacked by a guy wielding a samurai sword.

"He simply went at me and cut me three times," Mr Chinthana, the owner of a delivery firm, claimed.

Mr Chinthana stated that he had three injuries: a 10cm cut on his left shoulder, as well as minor wounds on his left hand and the region below his neck.

After charging at Mr Chinthana, the assailant, a 37-year-old male, stumbled and fell, pinning him to the ground. "He was really fortunate that the floor was slick and he fell. I was able to gain a firm grasp on his two legs and hold him down "Mr Chinthana, who sustained injuries on his elbows and knees as a result of the scuffle, agreed.

According to preliminary police investigations, the 37-year-old had eaten unknown medications before leaving his house with the sword. Following a short incident with members of the general public at Mr Chinthana told The Straits Times that he was on his way home after purchasing groceries for his pregnant wife when he noticed the man approximately 100 meters distant, wielding a sword and slashing vehicles on the road.

He was filming the situation on his phone when the man charged at him, causing him to defend himself with his grocery bags. "I'm not a fearful man; I don't know whether this is in my blood," said Mr Chinthana, who did not consider escaping despite the possibility of catastrophic injury. "I knew I could run, but I couldn't seem to get away."He added that once he pushed the man down, numerous other bystanders came to his rescue, and that the police arrived within five minutes.

Mr Chinthana was one of six persons honored by the Ang Mo Kio Police Division on Tuesday with the Public Spiritedness Award for detaining the armed assailant and preventing him from inflicting more harm to the public while waiting for the police to come. On Wednesday, the guy was accused in court with voluntarily inflicting harm with hazardous weapons or means.
