Indian England in West Indies: Mark Wood ruled out of second Test


Mark Wood has been ruled out of England's second Test against the West Indies in Barbados, and Saqib Mahmood will take his place. On the third day of the drew in the first Test in Antigua, Wood hurt his elbow. After feeling "severe discomfort" when trying to bowl in the nets before the start of play on day five, he did not bowl in West Indies' second innings. Mahmood, a 25-year-old Lancashire player, will make his Test debut on Wednesday. Ollie Robinson, who missed the first Test due to a back ailment, had a lengthy workout in the nets on Monday, is still unavailable. On the third day of the first Test, Wood was only able to bowl five overs before leaving the field. While both bowlers have been ruled out of the second Test, neither Wood nor Robinson have been ruled out of the three-match series finale. Wood's absence from England's squad will be "disappointing," according to England captain Joe Root. "He's been outstanding for us recently," Root remarked. "He'll be a key part of this squad in the future, but it offers other people a chance." Mahmood is a "nice alternative to have under our sleeve," according to Root. "For a guy who hasn't played a lot of international cricket, he's really mature and he understands how he wants to operate," Root said.

"He's been really remarkable." He'll provide us a point of distinction because he's on a somewhat different path. When he's played in various forms, he's done it before. "He clearly has outstanding control, especially when the ball rolls backwards." England's top two Test wicket-takers, James Anderson and Stuart Broad, were not included on the Caribbean trip. Neither player is thought to be on the reserve list, although Warwickshire's Liam Norwell, 30, is anticipated to be called up if necessary.
